## Partnership Overview

Oregon State University is a key academic partner of the Semio Community, bringing together expertise in robotics, human-robot interaction, and engineering to advance the field of social robotics and create accessible, impactful robotic technologies.

## Research Excellence

OSU's School of Mechanical, Industrial, and Manufacturing Engineering hosts world-class robotics research led by distinguished faculty:

### Dr. Bill Smart
- Pioneering work in mobile robotics and autonomous navigation
- Machine learning approaches for robust robot behavior
- Development of practical robotic systems for real-world deployment
- Extensive experience in robot software architecture and field robotics

### Dr. Naomi Fitter
- Leading research in socially assistive robotics
- Physical human-robot interaction studies
- Development of robots for health and wellness applications
- Innovative approaches to robot-mediated social engagement

## Key Contributions

### Research Areas
- **Mobile Robotics**: Advancing autonomous navigation in complex environments
- **Human-Robot Interaction**: Developing intuitive and effective interaction paradigms
- **Assistive Robotics**: Creating robots that support human health and wellbeing
- **Robot Learning**: Implementing machine learning techniques for adaptive robot behavior

### Educational Impact
OSU contributes to the Semio Community through:
- Training the next generation of roboticists
- Developing open educational resources
- Hosting workshops and research experiences
- Fostering interdisciplinary collaboration

## Collaborative Projects

The partnership between OSU and Semio Community enables:
- Shared research infrastructure and resources
- Joint development of robotics platforms and software
- Collaborative studies in human-robot interaction
- Knowledge transfer between academia and industry

## Future Directions

Oregon State University and Semio Community are working together to:
- Expand access to social robotics research tools
- Develop new standards for HRI research reproducibility
- Create inclusive robotics technologies
- Bridge the gap between research and real-world applications
